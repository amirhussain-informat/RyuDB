sql: EXISTS + uncorrelated scalar subqueries (Phase E-2)

Flatten uncorrelated scalar subqueries (single-row aggregates such as
SELECT COUNT(*)/MAX(...) FROM ...) and EXISTS (SELECT ...) into cross-joins
of a 1-row relation onto the outer plan, replacing the subquery node with a
bare Column reference to the broadcast column. Keeps eval_expr engine-free
and consistent with the E-1 IN/NOT IN lowering (the subquery is a normal
Join.right subtree the optimizer recurses into).

EXISTS becomes (SELECT COUNT(*) FROM (subq) LIMIT 1) > 0: 1 iff the subquery
has any row, else 0. Replacing exp.Exists with col > 0 (not a dedicated node)
means EXISTS works under AND/OR/NOT and in projection, not just as a top-level
WHERE conjunct (unlike IN). NOT EXISTS falls out via Not (the count is a
non-NULL int, so Not inverts it correctly).

Scalar subqueries are restricted to single-row aggregates (the only shape
with a guaranteed 1 row); non-aggregate and GROUP BY scalars are deferred.
Correlated scalar/EXISTS subqueries are rejected (deferred to E-3).

parse.py: _flatten_outer_subqueries runs in _build_select before the WHERE
block, collecting scalar/EXISTS subqueries from WHERE.this and sel.expressions
without descending into inner selects (owned by their own _build_select) or
IN-subqueries (owned by _apply_where_subqueries). Each is cross-joined onto
the running plan; the subquery node is replaced in place (exp.column / exp.GT)
so the replaced Columns flow into the residual Filter / projection normally.
_require_global_aggregate enforces the 1-row guarantee.

optimize.py: push_predicates must not push a conjunct that references a
non-base column (a cross-join broadcast column like _sq1) below a scan -- it
depends on a column produced above the join. Without this guard the
semi-join-wrapping-cross-join shape (scalar + IN in the same WHERE) mis-routes
the residual conjunct into a scan that lacks the broadcast column.
